MEDICATION ABSORPTION WINDOWS — The feature that destroys the competition
This is the one feature that a doctor on the jury will applaud. No app in the world does this correctly.
Build a hardcoded medication-food interaction table in your backend. Not AI-generated. A static lookup table. Cover the 12 most common Indian medications:
Metformin: must be taken with or after food. Schedule meal before medication window. Avoid high-sugar meals in the same slot.
Iron supplements (Ferrous Sulphate): avoid calcium-rich foods within 2 hours. Never pair with dairy, spinach, or tea in the same meal.
Levothyroxine (thyroid): must be taken 30–60 minutes before food. The first meal of the day must be scheduled accordingly.
Warfarin: avoid sudden increase in Vitamin K foods (leafy greens). The system monitors weekly leafy green frequency, not just individual meals.
Amlodipine (hypertension): avoid grapefruit entirely. Flag if recipe contains it.
When user inputs "Metformin with breakfast, Iron pill at night" — your backend parses this with regex, looks up the interaction table, and adds hard constraints to API Call 2: "Breakfast must contain food, not be empty. Dinner must not contain dairy, spinach, or tea. Iron supplement timing scheduled for 9 PM, so dinner at 7:30 PM latest."
This is deterministic logic. It cannot be wrong. It is medically defensible. Gemini never makes the medication decision — your code does.

Phase 1: Intelligent Onboarding & Regional Economics
Before the AI even touches a recipe, the system establishes a highly realistic financial baseline.
Regional Price Calibration: The app recognizes the user's location. Instead of starting from scratch, it auto-suggests a realistic baseline based on regional food inflation data (e.g., suggesting ₹7,260 for a family in Bokaro vs. ₹14,850 for Delhi).
Custom Input: The user can accept this regional average or type in any custom number for their monthly grocery budget.
Deep Clinical Profiling: The system logs each family member's specific medical conditions (e.g., diabetes, anemia), taste preferences, allergies, and crucially, active medications.
Phase 2: Deterministic Pre-Processing (The Math Guardrail)
To prevent the AI from hallucinating or blowing the budget, your backend code executes a rigid mathematical split in milliseconds before any AI generation begins.
The 10% Decoupled Buffer: 10% of the budget is instantly locked away for dry fruits and seasonal fresh fruits.
The 40% Fixed Staples: 40% is allocated for bulk, monthly dry goods (Atta, Rice, Dal, Oil).
The 50% Active Daily Budget: The remaining 50% is divided by 30 days to get the daily perishable limit (e.g., ₹166/day).
Weighted Meal Limits: The daily ₹166 is not divided equally by three. The code assigns a smaller weight to breakfast (e.g., ₹46) and larger weights to lunch and dinner (e.g., ₹60 each) to reflect the real-world cost of those meals.
Phase 3: The "Delta" Inventory Scan
The Virtual Pantry: Before generating lists, the app checks the user's logged inventory.
Zero-Waste Mandate: The system forces the upcoming AI generation to "burn down" existing perishable ingredients (e.g., leftover cabbage or paneer from last week) before adding new items to the shopping list.
Phase 4: The 3-Step Prompt Chain (The Engine)
The app runs three isolated API calls to keep the AI focused and accurate.
API Call 1 (Staples): Uses the 40% budget to generate a precise monthly bulk grocery list for the family size.
API Call 2 (Core Meals & Clinical Algorithms): Uses the 50% active budget, regional tags, pantry data, and medical profiles to generate the 21 weekly meals.
The "One Base, Many Plates" Rule: It generates one affordable main dish (e.g., Palak Dal & Roti) to keep costs low and kitchen efficiency high.
Plate-Level Instructions: It modifies that single base for each member (e.g., Papa gets Jowar roti and low salt; Mama gets added lemon).
Food-Drug Interoperability: The AI actively avoids pairing foods that block medication (e.g., ensuring calcium-heavy meals aren't scheduled when an iron supplement is taken).
API Call 3 (The Nutritional Bandaid): Uses the 10% Buffer budget. It suggests a monthly batch of shelf-stable dry fruits (e.g., almonds for the diabetic profile) and outputs a weekly line-item budget for "Seasonal Fresh Fruits" (e.g., ₹150) so the user can buy what is cheap locally.
Phase 5: The Trust-Building UI (The Waiting Room)
The user does not get an instant answer. They are placed in a 90 seconds generation screen to build trust in the complex calculations.
Transparent Status Updates: The screen cycles through exact processes:
"Calibrating regional food prices..."
"Scanning pantry for zero-waste utilization..."
"Calculating 1:4:5 budget splits for family of 4..."
"Cross-referencing active medications with ingredient macros..."
"Finalizing 'One Base, Many Plates' daily instructions..."
Phase 6: Dynamic Edge Cases (When Reality Hits)
The system is designed to bend, not break, when a user's schedule changes.
The Dining Out / Skipped Meal Scenario: If a user taps "Changed my mind" or goes out for dinner, the app does not recalculate the whole week.
The "Nutritional Bandaid" Deployment: To fix the macro-nutrient gap from the missed meal, the app simply pulls from the 10% Fruit/Dry Fruit buffer (e.g., "Missed your protein at lunch? Grab a handful of your monthly almonds now.").
Rolling Micro-Budgeting: The unused raw ingredients from that skipped meal are automatically pushed to the top of the queue for the next day, and any saved money rolls over, ensuring the ₹10,000 budget is strictly protected over the 30 days. 
THE GENERATION SCREEN — Reduce from 3-4 minutes to under 90 seconds
3-4 minutes at a live expo demo means the jury walks to the next stall. You lose Prototype Quality marks.
The pre-processing in your backend takes under 1 second. API Call 1 takes 8-15 seconds (Gemini). API Call 2 takes 15-25 seconds. API Call 3 takes 8-12 seconds. Total realistic time: 35-55 seconds.
Show real status updates with actual timings: "Resolving 3 conflicts in your family's health requirements... done (0.8s)" "Checking 5 pantry items for zero-waste priority... done (0.2s)" "Calculating medication absorption windows... done (0.3s)" "Generating monthly staples within ₹4,000... (12s)" "Building 21 meals with plate-level instructions... (22s)" "Optimising nutritional buffer for Dadi's fasting days... (10s)" "Plan ready."
The waiting screen is not fake. The timings are real. The specificity of the status messages is what builds trust — not the length of time.
Priority hierarchy, hardcoded in your backend:
Level 1 — allergy (absolute, never violated)
Level 2 — religious/cultural rule (absolute, never violated)
Level 3 — medication interaction window (absolute for the specific meal slot)
Level 4 — clinical condition management (diabetes, kidney, hypertension)
Level 5 — goal (weight loss, weight gain, muscle)
Level 6 — preference (spice, dislike)


When two members conflict at the same priority level — e.g., diabetic dad needs low-carb and underweight child needs high-calorie — the engine does not collapse. It resolves by: generating a low-carb base dish (satisfying level 4 for dad) and adding a calorie-dense side specifically for the child (satisfying level 5 for child). This is the plate-level instruction that goes to Gemini.
This conflict resolution step, with its reasoning, is shown to the user visually. "We detected 3 conflicts in your family's requirements. Here is how NutriNext resolved them." This transparency is what no app shows and what judges will remember.

THE HARMONY SCORE — Make it explainable, not just a number
The Family Harmony Score is not a vague output. It is calculated deterministically from the constraint graph.
Formula: Start at 100. For each unresolvable constraint (e.g., Jain member in a household that cooks non-veg), deduct 5 points. For each constraint resolved through plate modification rather than base dish change, deduct 2 points. For each constraint resolved at base dish level, deduct 0 points. For each pantry ingredient successfully used to avoid purchase, add 2 points. For each medication absorption window correctly scheduled, add 3 points.
Show the score breakdown: "91/100 — 6 points deducted: 4 from Dad's low-sodium requirement causing base dish restriction, 2 from Dadi's Tuesday fast requiring a full meal replacement. 3 points added for correct Metformin scheduling."
