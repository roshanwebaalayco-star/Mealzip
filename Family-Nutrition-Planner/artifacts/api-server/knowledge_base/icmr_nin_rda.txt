ICMR-NIN 2024 — RECOMMENDED DIETARY ALLOWANCES (RDA) FOR INDIANS
National Institute of Nutrition, Hyderabad
Nutrient Requirements and Recommended Dietary Allowances for Indians

ENERGY RDA — INDIANS BY AGE AND ACTIVITY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Adult Males (body weight reference: 65kg):
- Sedentary work (PAL 1.53): 2110 kcal/day
- Moderate activity (PAL 1.76): 2470 kcal/day
- Heavy activity (PAL 2.25): 3000 kcal/day

Adult Females (body weight reference: 55kg):
- Sedentary work (PAL 1.53): 1662 kcal/day
- Moderate activity (PAL 1.76): 1945 kcal/day
- Heavy activity (PAL 2.25): 2415 kcal/day

Special Physiological States:
- Pregnancy (2nd trimester): +350 kcal/day additional
- Pregnancy (3rd trimester): +350 kcal/day additional
- Lactation (0-6 months): +600 kcal/day additional
- Lactation (7-12 months): +520 kcal/day additional

Children (kcal/day):
- 1-3 years: 1060 kcal
- 4-6 years: 1350 kcal (boys), 1290 kcal (girls)
- 7-9 years: 1690 kcal (boys), 1610 kcal (girls)
- 10-12 years: 2060 kcal (boys), 1970 kcal (girls)
- 13-15 years: 2600 kcal (boys), 2330 kcal (girls)
- 16-18 years: 3000 kcal (boys), 2440 kcal (girls)

Elderly (60+ years):
- Males: 1900 kcal/day (sedentary), 2100 kcal/day (moderate)
- Females: 1600 kcal/day (sedentary), 1800 kcal/day (moderate)

PROTEIN RDA FOR INDIANS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Reference protein requirement: 0.83g/kg/day (egg protein digestibility equivalent)
Indian mixed diet digestibility correction factor: 0.90 (plant proteins less digestible)

Adult Males:
- Protein requirement: 54g/day (0.83g/kg × 65kg)
- Athletes/heavy workers: 1.2-1.6g/kg/day

Adult Females:
- Protein requirement: 46g/day (0.83g/kg × 55kg)
- Pregnancy: +24g/day additional = 70g/day total
- Lactation: +28g/day additional = 74g/day total

Children (g/day):
- 1-3 years: 12.5g
- 4-6 years: 16.7g
- 7-9 years: 23.6g
- 10-12 years: 32.3g (boys), 31.5g (girls)
- 13-15 years: 45.5g (boys), 43.0g (girls)
- 16-18 years: 55.4g (boys), 45.2g (girls)

High-protein Indian foods (protein per 100g):
- Soybean: 43g protein — highest plant protein source
- Peanuts (groundnuts): 25.3g protein — affordable, complete amino acids
- Masoor dal (lentils, raw): 25.1g protein
- Moong dal (raw): 24.5g protein
- Chana dal (split chickpeas): 20.8g protein
- Paneer: 18.3g protein
- Eggs: 13.3g protein (complete protein, excellent bioavailability)
- Low-fat dahi: 11g protein
- Fish (rohu): 16.6g protein
- Chicken (skinless): 25.9g protein

IRON RDA FOR INDIANS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Iron bioavailability from Indian diet: 8-12% (non-haeme iron, plant-based)
Iron bioavailability from flesh foods: 20-25% (haeme iron, better absorbed)

Adult Males:
- Iron RDA: 17mg/day

Adult Females:
- Pre-menopausal: 21mg/day (menstrual losses account for higher need)
- Post-menopausal: 17mg/day
- Pregnancy: 27mg/day (increased blood volume + fetal needs)
- Lactation: 17mg/day

Children:
- 1-3 years: 9mg/day
- 4-6 years: 13mg/day
- 7-9 years: 16mg/day
- 10-12 years: 21mg/day (girls, onset of menstruation)
- 13-15 years: 27mg/day (girls)
- 16-18 years: 26mg/day (girls)

Iron content in Indian foods (mg per 100g):
- Moringa leaves (drumstick): 25.0mg — exceptional source
- Sesame seeds (til): 14.6mg
- Bajra (pearl millet, raw): 8.0mg
- Rajma (kidney beans, raw): 6.4mg
- Masoor dal (lentils, raw): 7.6mg
- Jaggery (gud): 11.4mg — iron-rich alternative to sugar
- Spinach (palak, cooked): 2.7mg
- Amaranth leaves (chaulai): 3.9mg
- Chicken liver: 9.5mg (haeme iron — excellent bioavailability)
- Eggs: 2.0mg (haeme iron)

Iron absorption enhancers (include with iron-rich meals):
- Vitamin C (amla, lemon, guava, tomato): increases non-haeme iron absorption 2-3 fold
- Fermentation (idli, dosa, dhokla): reduces phytates — improves iron absorption

Iron absorption inhibitors (avoid with iron-rich foods):
- Tea and coffee (tannins): reduce iron absorption by 50-60%
- Calcium-rich foods (milk, dahi): compete with iron absorption
- Phytates in whole grains: reduce plant iron absorption (fermentation reduces phytates)

CALCIUM RDA FOR INDIANS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Adult Males and Non-Pregnant Females: 1000mg/day
Pregnant and Lactating Women: 1200mg/day
Children (4-9 years): 800mg/day
Children (10-17 years): 1000mg/day
Elderly (60+): 1000mg/day

Calcium bioavailability:
- Dairy (milk, dahi): 30-40% absorbed — best source
- Ragi: 25-30% absorbed (oxalate binds some calcium, but quantity is high)
- Dark green leafy vegetables: 10-20% absorbed (oxalates in spinach reduce absorption)
- Sesame seeds (til): 20-25% absorbed

Calcium content in Indian foods (mg per 100g):
- Ragi (finger millet): 344mg — highest of all cereals
- Sesame seeds (til): 975mg — excellent but consumed in small quantity
- Milk (cow): 118mg per 100ml
- Paneer: 208mg
- Dahi (low-fat): 120mg
- Amaranth leaves (chaulai): 395mg
- Moringa leaves: 440mg
- Radish leaves (mooli patte): 265mg

Vitamin D is essential for calcium absorption:
- Deficiency extremely common in India (despite sun exposure — due to skin pigmentation, clothing)
- Recommended: 600 IU/day for adults, 800 IU/day for elderly
- Sources: sunlight (primary), eggs, fish, fortified milk, mushrooms

VITAMIN AND MICRONUTRIENT RDA — KEY VALUES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Vitamin A (RAE mcg/day):
- Adult males: 1000 mcg RAE
- Adult females: 840 mcg RAE
- Children 4-9: 400-600 mcg RAE
- Vitamin A deficiency: night blindness, xerophthalmia (common in India)
- Sources: liver, eggs, dairy, orange-yellow vegetables (beta-carotene), dark green leafy vegetables

Vitamin C (mg/day):
- Adults: 80mg/day
- Pregnant: 100mg/day
- Lactating: 120mg/day
- Best Indian sources: amla (600mg/100g), guava (228mg/100g), lemon (53mg/100g)

Vitamin B12 (mcg/day):
- Adults: 2.2 mcg/day
- Pregnant: 2.6 mcg/day
- Lactating: 2.8 mcg/day
- Critical note: Vitamin B12 is found ONLY in animal foods (meat, fish, eggs, dairy)
- Vegetarian adults and especially elderly vegetarians are at HIGH RISK of B12 deficiency
- B12 deficiency causes: megaloblastic anaemia, neurological damage, cognitive decline
- Supplementation: recommended for strict vegetarians, especially children and elderly

Folate / Folic Acid (mcg DFE/day):
- Adults: 220 mcg DFE
- Pregnant: 600 mcg DFE (critical for neural tube defect prevention)
- Lactating: 350 mcg DFE
- Indian sources: green leafy vegetables, legumes, liver, eggs

Zinc (mg/day):
- Adult males: 17mg
- Adult females: 13.2mg
- Children 4-8: 5-8mg
- Zinc deficiency: stunting in children, immune dysfunction, poor wound healing
- Sources: whole grains, legumes, pumpkin seeds, meat, shellfish

Iodine (mcg/day):
- Adults: 150 mcg
- Pregnant: 250 mcg
- Lactating: 250 mcg
- Iodine deficiency: goitre, hypothyroidism, cretinism
- India mandate: iodized salt in all households (Universal Salt Iodization programme)

MACRONUTRIENT DISTRIBUTION RANGES (AMDR)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

As percentage of total energy:
- Carbohydrates: 50-60%
- Protein: 12-15%
- Total fat: 20-30%
  - Saturated fat: < 10%
  - MUFA: 10-15%
  - PUFA: 8-10%
  - Trans fat: < 1%
- Dietary fibre: 25-40g/day (minimum 14g per 1000 kcal)
- Added sugar: < 10% of total energy (< 5% for stricter health benefits)
- Salt: < 5g/day (< 2g sodium)
