WEIGHT LOSS — NORTH INDIA PATTERN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Goal: Weight loss, caloric deficit 300-500 kcal/day
Region: North India (Punjab, Haryana, UP, Delhi, Himachal Pradesh)
Daily calorie target: 1400-1600 kcal
Protein target: 60-70g/day
Carbohydrate: 160-180g/day
Fat: 40-50g/day

Early Morning (6:00-7:00 AM):
- 1 glass warm lemon water (0 kcal)
- 4-5 soaked almonds or 2 walnuts

Breakfast (8:00-9:00 AM):
- 2 multigrain rotis (besan + gehun atta, no maida) = 180 kcal
- 1 bowl moong dal / chana dal = 150 kcal
- 1 cup low-fat dahi = 60 kcal
- 1 cucumber + 1 tomato salad

Mid-Morning Snack (11:00 AM):
- 1 seasonal fruit (guava, amla, papaya preferred)
- 1 glass buttermilk (chaas) without cream

Lunch (1:00-2:00 PM):
- 2 bajra or jowar roti
- 1 bowl mixed sabzi (palak, methi, lauki, tinda — low calorie vegetables)
- 1 bowl rajma or chana (protein source)
- Salad: onion, cucumber, tomato, lemon
- 1 bowl thin dal

Evening Snack (4:00-5:00 PM):
- 1 cup green tea or herbal tea (no sugar)
- 1 handful roasted chana or makhana (fox nuts)

Dinner (7:00-8:00 PM):
- 2 thin multigrain rotis
- 1 bowl sabzi (light, no cream or butter)
- 1 bowl low-fat dahi

Avoid: Puri, parantha with butter, ghee-heavy curries, white rice, maida, fried snacks, packaged foods.
Prefer: Steamed, sautéed, boiled preparations. Mustard oil in small quantity (5ml/meal) is acceptable.

WEIGHT LOSS — SOUTH INDIA PATTERN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Goal: Weight loss, caloric deficit 300-500 kcal/day
Region: South India (Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana)
Daily calorie target: 1400-1600 kcal

Early Morning:
- Warm water with lemon or 1 tsp turmeric in warm water

Breakfast (7:00-8:00 AM):
- 2 idli (steamed, small) = 130 kcal each
- 1 bowl sambar (no coconut, add more vegetables) = 100 kcal
- 1 tbsp green chutney (tomato-onion base, no coconut)
- OR: 1 bowl oats upma with vegetables

Mid-Morning:
- 1 glass buttermilk (neer mor)
- 1 guava or seasonal fruit

Lunch (12:30-1:30 PM):
- 1 cup brown rice or 1 cup hand-pounded rice = 200 kcal
- 1 bowl rasam (tomato/tamarind-based, high iron, vitamin C)
- 1 bowl kootu or poriyal (mixed vegetables)
- 1 bowl sambar
- Small piece of fish (if non-veg) for omega-3 and protein

Evening Snack:
- Roasted groundnuts or 1 boiled egg
- 1 cup green tea

Dinner (7:00 PM):
- 2 small dosa (no oil, or minimal sesame oil) with sambar
- OR 1 cup brown rice + 1 bowl dal

Avoid: White rice in excess, coconut milk curries, fried vada/bajji, sweet pongal, sweet beverages.
Prefer: Steamed idli, dosa with minimal oil, more rasam-based dishes, dark leafy vegetable poriyal.

DIABETES MANAGEMENT — ALL INDIA PATTERN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Goal: Blood glucose control, HbA1c reduction
Condition: Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM)
Daily calorie: 1600-1800 kcal (adjust per BMI and activity)
Carbohydrate restriction: 45-55% of total calories (180-220g/day)
Glycaemic Index (GI): Prefer low GI foods (GI < 55)
Protein target: 1.0-1.2g/kg body weight
Fibre: minimum 25-30g/day

Meal timing is crucial — eat every 2-3 hours to prevent blood sugar spikes.

Early Morning (before 7 AM):
- 1 tsp methi (fenugreek) seeds soaked overnight, eat seeds and drink water
- This helps insulin sensitivity — clinical evidence from ICMR studies

Breakfast (7:30-8:00 AM) — Low GI:
- 2 multigrain roti (mixed: whole wheat 50%, besan 25%, jowar 25%)
- 1 bowl moong dal chilla (high protein, low GI)
- OR 1 bowl daliya (broken wheat porridge) with vegetables — GI 41
- 1 cup green tea or black tea (no sugar)
- Avoid: white bread, poha (high GI), white rice, fruit juices, corn flakes

Mid-Morning (10:30 AM):
- 1 small guava or jamun (excellent for diabetes — GI 28-56)
- OR 10-12 soaked almonds
- Avoid: banana, mango, chiku in large quantity (high GI)

Lunch (12:30-1:00 PM) — Structured plate method:
- 1/2 plate: non-starchy vegetables (palak, bhindi, gobi, methi, lauki, tinda)
- 1/4 plate: complex carbohydrate (1 cup brown rice OR 2 jowar/bajra roti)
- 1/4 plate: protein (1 bowl dal, 1 bowl curd, or 100g fish/chicken — no red meat)
- Add: 1 bowl thin rasam or clear soup (digestive + low calorie)
- Salad: cucumber, tomato, raw onion with lemon + rock salt

Afternoon Snack (4:00 PM):
- 1 small bowl roasted makhana (fox nuts) — low GI, high protein
- OR 1 cup buttermilk (chaas, no sugar)
- Avoid: biscuits, namkeen, chips, packaged foods

Dinner (7:00 PM — eat before 8:30 PM):
- 2 jowar or bajra roti (lower GI than wheat)
- 1 bowl mixed vegetable sabzi (light, no cream)
- 1 bowl low-fat dahi (probiotic benefit)
- Walk 15 minutes after dinner (reduces post-meal glucose spike by 20-30% per research)

Key Foods for Diabetes — ICMR Endorsed:
- Bitter gourd (karela): reduces blood glucose
- Methi leaves and seeds: lowers blood glucose, improves insulin sensitivity
- Jamun (black plum): antidiabetic properties
- Amla (Indian gooseberry): vitamin C, helps glycemic control
- Cinnamon (dalchini): improves insulin sensitivity
- Turmeric (haldi): anti-inflammatory, supports glucose metabolism

Avoid Completely: White sugar, maida products (samosa, puri), white rice in large quantity, packaged sweets, soft drinks, fruit juices, alcohol.

WEIGHT GAIN — EAST INDIA PATTERN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Goal: Healthy weight gain, muscle mass increase
Region: East India (West Bengal, Odisha, Assam, Bihar, Jharkhand)
Daily calorie target: 2800-3200 kcal (caloric surplus 400-600 kcal)
Protein: 1.5-2.0g/kg body weight
Carbohydrate: 55-65% of calories

Early Morning (6:00 AM):
- 1 glass full-fat milk with banana + 1 tsp honey

Breakfast (7:30-8:00 AM):
- 2-3 parantha (wheat) with ghee or mustard oil = 350-450 kcal
- 2 scrambled eggs or 1 cup paneer bhurji = 200 kcal
- 1 glass full-fat dahi
- OR (vegetarian): 1 bowl luchi with aloo dum (traditional Bengali)

Mid-Morning Snack (10:30 AM):
- 1 glass lassi (full fat, with banana)
- OR 1 banana + handful mixed nuts and seeds

Lunch (1:00 PM):
- 2 cups white rice
- 1 bowl masoor dal (rich in protein, iron)
- 1 bowl sabzi (aloo posto, begun bhaja, or seasonal)
- 1 piece fish (hilsa/rohu/catla — high omega-3, protein)
- 1 piece papad
- Dessert: 1 small piece mishti doi or sandesh (occasional, controlled)

Afternoon Snack (4:30 PM):
- 2-3 khajur (dates) with 10-12 cashews = 300 kcal
- OR 1 glass peanut lassi

Dinner (7:30-8:00 PM):
- 2 cups rice or 3-4 roti
- 1 bowl macher jhol (fish curry — light) or chicken curry
- 1 bowl shukto or mixed vegetable
- 1 glass full-fat milk before bed

Pre-Sleep:
- 1 glass warm turmeric milk (haldi doodh) = 200 kcal

Focus on calorie-dense whole foods: ghee, mustard oil, full-fat dairy, fish, eggs, legumes, rice.
Avoid: empty calories from chips, soft drinks, maida fried snacks.

ANAEMIA MANAGEMENT — ALL INDIA PATTERN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Goal: Increase haemoglobin, treat iron-deficiency anaemia
Target: Haemoglobin > 12 g/dL (women), > 13 g/dL (men)
Daily iron target: 17-21mg/day (RDA per ICMR-NIN 2024)
Vitamin C: essential for non-haeme iron absorption — take with iron-rich foods

Iron-Rich Foods (prioritise daily):
- Palak (spinach): 2.7mg per 100g
- Methi leaves: 1.9mg per 100g
- Chaulai (amaranth): high iron, vitamin C
- Horse gram (kulthi dal): 7mg per 100g
- Rajma: 6.4mg per 100g
- Masoor dal: 7.6mg per 100g
- Til (sesame seeds): 14.6mg per 100g
- Jaggery (gud): 11.4mg per 100g — better than sugar for anaemia
- Dates: 0.9mg per 100g
- Moringa (drumstick leaves): 25mg per 100g — exceptional iron source

Early Morning:
- Amla juice (50ml) — high vitamin C, enhances iron absorption
- Soaked raisins (10-12) — good iron source

Breakfast:
- Palak or methi parantha (2 pieces) — iron rich green leafy vegetable
- 1 boiled egg (for non-veg) — haeme iron, better absorption
- 1 glass amla juice or orange juice (vitamin C with breakfast enhances iron absorption by 2-3x)

Mid-Morning:
- 1 small bowl mixed sprouts (moong + chana + moth) with lemon — iron + vitamin C

Lunch:
- 1 bowl palak dal (spinach + lentil) — iron powerhouse
- 1 bowl cooked horse gram (kulthi) or rajma
- 1 cup brown rice
- 1 piece lemon squeezed over food (vitamin C)
- Raw salad with amla or tomato

Evening Snack:
- 1 tsp chyawanprash with warm water
- Handful til-gud ladoo (sesame-jaggery) — traditional high-iron snack
- 1 cup green tea (space away from meals — tannins inhibit iron absorption)

Dinner:
- 2 bajra roti (bajra has iron)
- 1 bowl chaulai sabzi (amaranth leaves)
- 1 bowl masoor dal

Important: Avoid tea/coffee with meals — tannins reduce iron absorption by 50-60%.
Avoid: Calcium supplements with iron-rich meals (calcium inhibits iron absorption).

CHILDREN (4-12 YEARS) — GROWTH AND DEVELOPMENT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Goal: Optimal growth, brain development, strong immunity
Age group: 4-12 years
Daily calorie: 1200-1800 kcal (age dependent)
Protein: 20-40g/day
Calcium: 600-800mg/day (critical for bone development)
Iron: 12-15mg/day (prevents anaemia affecting school performance)

Breakfast (mandatory — never skip):
- 1 glass full-fat milk with banana and 1 tsp honey = 200 kcal
- 2 small idli with sambar or 1 bowl upma with vegetables
- OR 2 whole wheat toast with peanut butter (homemade, no additives)
- Fruit: 1 guava or amla or seasonal fruit

School Lunch:
- 2 small roti with sabzi
- 1 small bowl dal
- 1 small bowl curd
- Seasonal fruit
- Avoid: processed packaged foods, chips, biscuits, namkeen, carbonated drinks

After School Snack:
- 1 glass warm milk
- Groundnut chikki or til laddoo (traditional — iron, calcium, protein)
- Sprouted chana chaat

Dinner:
- 2 roti with dal and sabzi
- 1 glass warm turmeric milk before sleep (calcium, immunity)

Key Nutrients for Children per ICMR-NIN 2024:
- Calcium for bone: milk, dahi, ragi, sesame seeds, green leafy vegetables
- Iron for brain and blood: dal, green leafy, jaggery, moringa
- Zinc for immunity and growth: whole grains, legumes, pumpkin seeds
- Omega-3 for brain: fish (2-3 times/week), walnuts, flaxseed (alsi)
- Vitamin A: carrots, sweet potato, papaya, mango, orange-yellow vegetables

Avoid: Fast food, ultra-processed snacks, sugary beverages, excess salt and sugar.

SENIOR CITIZENS (60+ YEARS) — HEALTHY AGEING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Goal: Prevent malnutrition, maintain muscle mass, bone health
Age: 60+ years
Daily calorie: 1600-1800 kcal (metabolic rate decreases)
Protein: 1.0-1.2g/kg body weight (critical to prevent sarcopenia)
Calcium: 1000mg/day (osteoporosis prevention)
Vitamin D: 10-15 mcg/day (from sunlight + diet)
Vitamin B12: crucial (deficiency common in elderly vegetarians)

Morning:
- 10-15 minutes sunlight exposure (Vitamin D synthesis)
- 1 glass warm water
- 1 tsp isabgol (psyllium husk) in warm water (prevents constipation)

Breakfast:
- 2 small idli or 1 bowl daliya (easy to digest, soft)
- 1 cup warm milk with turmeric
- 1 small banana or soft fruit (guava, papaya)

Mid-Morning:
- 1 bowl soft dal soup
- OR 1 glass lassi (low fat, probiotic)

Lunch:
- 2 soft roti (can add flaxseed/alsi for omega-3)
- 1 bowl sabzi (soft cooked — aloo, lauki, tinda, carrot)
- 1 bowl dal (protein critical for elderly)
- 1 small bowl dahi
- 1 glass chaas

Afternoon:
- 1 glass warm milk
- 2-3 dates or 1 small piece jaggery (iron, calcium)
- 10 almonds (soaked, peeled — easy to chew)

Dinner (light — before 7:30 PM):
- 1-2 soft roti
- 1 bowl light dal
- 1 bowl soft sabzi

Key Considerations for Elderly:
- Vitamin B12 (cobalamin): vegetarian seniors at high risk — fortified foods or supplements
- Calcium + Vitamin D together for bone health
- Reduce salt to < 5g/day (hypertension prevention)
- Adequate hydration (elderly often underhydrate)
- Smaller, more frequent meals (digestion slows with age)

HYPERTENSION — BLOOD PRESSURE MANAGEMENT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Goal: Reduce blood pressure, support DASH-India pattern
Condition: Hypertension (BP > 130/80 mmHg)
Salt restriction: < 5g/day (ICMR-NIN recommendation) = < 2g sodium
Potassium: increase (helps counter sodium) — from fruits and vegetables

DASH-India Approach (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension):
- High in fruits, vegetables, whole grains
- Low in saturated fat, sodium, processed foods
- Moderate dairy (low fat)

Daily Pattern:
- Breakfast: 2 multigrain roti + green chutney + 1 glass nimbu paani (no salt)
- Lunch: 1 bowl brown rice + rajma or moong dal + palak sabzi
- Dinner: 2 jowar roti + dal + mixed vegetable sabzi

High Potassium Foods (include daily):
- Banana (1 medium = 422mg potassium)
- Sweet potato (1 cup = 950mg potassium)
- Coconut water (natural electrolyte balance)
- Amla
- Spinach (palak)
- Tomatoes

Reduce or Avoid:
- Pickles (achar) — very high sodium
- Packaged namkeen, chips, biscuits
- Processed meats
- Excess ghee and saturated fat
- Alcohol

BUDGET MEAL PATTERN — LOW INCOME HOUSEHOLDS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Goal: Complete nutrition at < ₹100 per person per day
All India pattern — maximise nutrition per rupee

Most Nutritious Low-Cost Foods (ICMR-NIN endorsed):
- Masoor dal: ₹60-80/kg, 25g protein/100g, 7.6mg iron
- Chana dal: ₹70-90/kg, complete protein
- Peanuts (groundnut): ₹80-100/kg, 25g protein, healthy fat
- Eggs: ₹6-8 each, complete protein, B12, vitamin D
- Ragi (finger millet): ₹50-60/kg, calcium powerhouse (344mg/100g)
- Seasonal vegetables: ₹20-40/kg (lauki, tinda, beans, palak)
- Amla: ₹20-40/kg, highest vitamin C, iron absorption enhancer
- Jaggery (gud): ₹50-60/kg, iron, calcium — better than sugar

Sample Budget Day (₹60-80 per person):
- Breakfast: Ragi daliya (porridge) with jaggery = ₹10
- Lunch: Masoor dal + rice + lauki sabzi + onion salad = ₹25
- Snack: Roasted peanuts + 1 seasonal fruit (amla/guava) = ₹10
- Dinner: Chana dal + 2 bajra roti + stir-fried greens = ₹20

Avoid in Budget Planning:
- Packaged convenience foods (nutritionally poor, expensive per calorie)
- Excess sugar and white flour products
- Soft drinks and packaged beverages (zero nutrition, high cost)

FASTING PATTERN — NAVRATRI / EKADASHI
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Goal: Maintain nutrition during religious fasting
Allowed foods during fasting (Satvik):
- Sabudana (tapioca pearls)
- Kuttu (buckwheat) — high protein, iron
- Singhara atta (water chestnut flour)
- Sama ke chawal (barnyard millet) — GI 50, diabetic friendly
- Rajgira (amaranth) — exceptional calcium and iron
- Fresh fruits
- Milk, curd, paneer
- Sendha namak (rock salt — only salt allowed in fast)
- Dry fruits and nuts

Fasting Meal Pattern:
- Morning: 1 glass warm water + soaked dry fruits
- Breakfast: 1 bowl sabudana khichdi with peanuts (not for diabetics — high GI)
  OR kuttu ki roti with dahi (better for diabetics)
- Lunch: 1 bowl sama rice + 1 bowl aloo sabzi + curd
  OR rajgira roti with paneer sabzi
- Snack: 1 glass fresh fruit juice or whole fruit + handful makhana
- Dinner: Kuttu ka dosa or rajgira roti + dahi

Important for Diabetics During Fasting:
- Avoid sabudana (GI 85 — causes blood sugar spike)
- Prefer sama ke chawal (GI 50) and kuttu (GI 54)
- Include protein at every meal (paneer, curd, peanuts)
- Monitor blood sugar more frequently during fasting

RECOMMENDED DIETARY ALLOWANCES (RDA) FOR INDIANS:
Source: ICMR-NIN 2024 Dietary Guidelines for Indians

Energy (kcal/day):
- Adult male (sedentary): 2110 kcal
- Adult male (moderate activity): 2470 kcal
- Adult female (sedentary): 1662 kcal
- Adult female (moderate activity): 1945 kcal
- Pregnant: +350 kcal above normal
- Lactating: +600 kcal above normal
- Children 7-9 years: 1690-1950 kcal
- Elderly 60+: 1800-2000 kcal

Protein (g/day):
- Adult male: 54g/day (0.83g/kg body weight)
- Adult female: 46g/day
- Pregnant: 78g/day
- Lactating: 74g/day
- Children 4-6 years: 20g/day
- Children 7-9 years: 29g/day

Iron (mg/day):
- Adult male: 17mg
- Adult female (pre-menopausal): 21mg
- Pregnant: 27mg
- Children 4-6 years: 13mg
- Children 7-9 years: 16mg

Calcium (mg/day):
- Adults: 1000mg
- Pregnant / Lactating: 1200mg
- Children 4-9 years: 800mg
- Elderly 60+: 1000mg

Vitamin C (mg/day):
- Adults: 80mg
- Pregnant: 100mg
- Lactating: 120mg

The Indian plate should consist of:
- 50% vegetables and fruits
- 25% whole grains and millets
- 25% protein (dal, legumes, dairy, eggs, fish, lean meat)
- Healthy fats: 3-4 tsp (mustard oil / groundnut oil / coconut oil — rotate)
- Minimize: sugar (< 10% of total energy), salt (< 5g/day), saturated fat

Millets promotion per ICMR-NIN 2024 (International Year of Millets 2023):
- Bajra (pearl millet): iron, magnesium, good for diabetes, heart
- Jowar (sorghum): calcium, B vitamins, gluten-free
- Ragi (finger millet): highest calcium of all cereals (344mg/100g), iron
- Rajgira (amaranth): complete protein, calcium, iron
- Kuttu (buckwheat): high protein, magnesium, low GI
